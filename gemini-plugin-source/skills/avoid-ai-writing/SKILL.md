# Avoid AI Writing

Use this skill to audit and rewrite content to remove common "AI-isms" and artificial patterns, making the prose feel more natural and human.

## When to Use
- Auditing content generated by LLMs (blog posts, emails, documentation).
- Refining technical writing to be more direct and less "flowery."
- Removing repetitive AI structures or specific vocabulary that "tells" it's AI-generated.

## How it Works
The skill identifies specific categories of AI-isms and provides rules for rewriting them into natural human prose.

### 1. Language & Vocabulary
- **Replace "AI-isms" with Plain English:** Avoid overused "tier 1" words.
  - *Leverage/Utilize* → **Use**
  - *Robust* → **Reliable**
  - *Seamless* → **Smooth**
  - *Commence* → **Start**
  - *Ascertain* → **Find out**
- **Avoid Copula Avoidance:** Don't use fancy verbs to describe simple existence.
  - *Avoid:* "serves as," "features," "boasts," "presents."
  - *Use:* "is," "has."
- **Eliminate Filler & Template Phrases:**
  - *Avoid:* "In order to," "Due to the fact that," "Moreover," "Furthermore."
  - *Use:* "To," "Because," "And," "Also."
- **Stop Synonym Cycling:** AI often cycles through "developers," "engineers," "practitioners," and "builders" in one paragraph. Pick the clearest word and stick to it.

### 2. Content & Tone
- **Deflate Significance:** Avoid "watershed moments," "pivotal shifts," or "marking a new era." Stick to specific facts.
- **Be Specific with Attributions:** Replace "Experts believe" or "Studies show" with specific citations.
- **Remove Promotional Fluff:** Cut adjectives like "vibrant," "nestled," "thriving," or "breathtaking" unless they add necessary information.
- **Avoid "Superficial -ing" Analysis:** Phrases like "symbolizing...", "reflecting...", or "showcasing..." are often hollow. Replace them with concrete facts or delete them.

### 3. Structural Rules
- **Vary Sentence Rhythm:** AI tends to produce uniform sentence lengths (15–25 words). Mix short sentences, long sentences, and even fragments.
- **Fix Formatting Overuse:**
  - Avoid excessive **bolding** and bullet-heavy lists.
  - Use sentence case for headings instead of Title Case.
  - Replace em dashes (—) with commas or periods where appropriate.
- **Lead with the Point:** Don't use "What if..." rhetorical questions or "Here are 7 reasons why..." intros. State the claim or the most important fact immediately.
- **Avoid Inline-Header Lists:** Instead of "**Speed:** Speed improved by...", just write the prose: "The platform is faster because..."

### 4. Communication & "Chatbot" Artifacts
- **Remove Openers/Closers:** Delete "Certainly!", "I hope this helps!", "In conclusion," and "The future looks bright."
- **Cut "Let's" Constructions:** Avoid "Let's explore," "Let's dive in," or "Let's break this down." Just start the explanation.
- **Eliminate Sycophancy:** Remove "Great question!" or "You're absolutely right!"
- **Avoid Reasoning Artifacts:** Don't say "Let me think step by step" or "To answer your question." Just provide the answer.

### 5. The "Human" Touch
- **Keep Natural Disfluency:** Don't sand away every irregularity. Perfectly uniform, over-polished prose is a major AI tell.
- **Earn the Emotion:** Avoid "I was fascinated to discover" or "What surprised me most." Let the facts create the interest rather than claiming it.

## Examples

### AI-Generated (Before)
"In the vibrant landscape of modern tech, companies must leverage robust solutions to ensure a seamless experience. Furthermore, it is important to note that this marks a watershed moment for the industry."

### Natural (After)
"Tech companies need reliable tools to keep things running smoothly. This is a big change for the industry."
